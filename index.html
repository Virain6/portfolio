About Me

I am an Engineering Student at Western University dedicated to my work while maintaining many interests outside school. In London, Ontario, I keep myself on a balanced diet and attempt to exercise every day while ensuring I do not fall behind on my studies. I have developed many interests that allowed me to showcase my collaboration skills on many occasions. One of my primary interests is playing basketball in a miniature community league with some of my peers every Monday. Another leading hobby I pursue is lyricism and poetry with my roommates. While living in off-campus housing, my roommates and I realized we all share an interest in making music. We all began investing time in our hobby in hopes of making impactful music one day. We are working toward refining individual skills while educating each other about our outstanding skills. For example, I help my peers with the writing process while one roommate is highly skilled with production and the other has practice with vocal control. My hometown is near the "Greater Toronto Area" (GTA), meaning most activities I am involved in are there. Last summer, I gained experience at a nonprofit organization (NPO), CAROBOT, as an engineer trainee. Our tasks included designing and constructing affordable educational kits for middle school students. The purpose of these kits was to get youths introduced to technology. These kits would arrive disassembled with extra parts, allowing students to tinker with the components. Following our guidelines, students get familiar with electrical components such as a breadboard, transistor, and LED. Students would code small chips like the Arduino Uno and ESP8266 to gain software experience. In my adolescence, I would've appreciated the opportunity to get exposed to such technology; however, due to circumstances including limited finances and my family's lack of understanding of technology. While working on something I was passionate about, I tested my skills in a real-world setting. To complete the project, the employees worked as a group. In this environment, my leadership skills were tested in real-world settings. My tasks involved creating timelines and ensuring employees met deadlines while providing them the support they needed. Throughout the work term, I improved my collaboration skills as we all worked long hours on some nights to finish the projects we put a notable amount of effort into. This experience provided me with the opportunity to understand the necessity of teamwork. Even though I strongly believe in solving problems by oneself, one must be humble enough to ask for assistance. In some situations, it may seem that solving problems alone is much quicker; however, on many occasions, teamwork is much more effective. Understanding each other's strengths is imperative for working efficiently like a well-made machine. This summer, I revisited the construction job I had worked in my previous years. As one of the more experienced employees, my responsibilities included guiding new employees while still meeting deadlines. Conflicts were common, and I was required to resolve them before my superiors believed it to be a genuine issue. Now, I can confidently say these past two years have been crucial for my personal and professional growth. Being surrounded by colleagues who value teamwork, clear communication, and mutual respect, I was able to leave my workplace as a superior person.
